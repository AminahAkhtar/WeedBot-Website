    • Biography

    Dr. Muhammad Khurram is a Professor at the Ned University of Engineering & Technology and also serves as Co-chair of the Computer and Information Systems Engineering Department. He also holds a regional directorate position in the National Center for Artificial Intelligence (NCAI). In addition to being Principal Investigator of Smart City Lab, an NCAI-affiliated research center, he is also serving as a director of the Research Center for Artificial Intelligence (RCAI) . Prior to that, he was the regional head of the Kosher Foundation, a non-profit organisation based in the United States. His expertise and contributions are in the areas of AI, IoT, computer vision, on-chip bio-systems, embedded systems, digital signal processing, and VLSI system design.

    Career

    He has 18+ years of academic experience with leading various R&D projects. Muhammad Khurram has Produced two Phd graduates and currently Supervising Four (02) PhD Students in the field of On-chip biological system modelling, neuromorphic hardware design and 5G Networks. As well as Supervising various research-based projects at postgraduate and undergraduate levels. He has earned his Bachelor's and Master's degree in Computer and Information Systems from NED University of Engineering & Technology. Ph.D. in science from Massey University in New Zealand.
