WEEEDS / CROPS

Bitter Gourd

Another commonly produced vegetable, bitter gourd is prone to different types of weed which can significantly reduce yield.

Herb Paris

Herb-Paris has four oval leaves set in a cross, with an understated crown of yellow-green flowers rising from the middle. This makes it quite a distinctive plant of ancient and damp woodlands on chalky soils.

Sponge Gourd

Sponge gourd is cultivated in different parts of Pakistan including Punjab and Sindh. It is also known as “Luffa” sponge gourd. The vines of sponge gourd attain the height of 30 feet or more.

Small Weed

A weed is a plant considered undesirable in a particular situation, "a plant in the wrong place", or a plant growing where it is not wanted. This introduces the concept of human and their goal in any particular setting

Okra

In Pakistan, okra is grown on an estimated 2.21105 hectares of land and produces 2.86 106 tonnes of green pods. It is highly consumed by common locals.

Grass

Grass, any of many low, green, nonwoody plants belonging to the grass family (Poaceae), the sedge family (Cyperaceae), and the rush family (Juncaceae). There are many grasslike members of other flowering plant families, but only the approximately 10,000 species in the family Poaceae are true grasses.

Papaya

Papaya is widely cultivated in different areas of pakistan along with the coastline of sindh and balochistan. It is cultivated and consumed extensively due to its medicinal, nutritional, and pharmacological properties.

Horse Weed

Horseweed, also called mare's tail, is a summer annual or biennial broadleaf plant. It is found throughout California to about 6600 feet (2000 m) and inhabits agricultural land, disturbed managed sites, and landscaped areas.
